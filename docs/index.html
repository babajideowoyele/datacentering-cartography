// 01   The problem

Strategic invisibility is a sociotechnical fact

Operators strategically conceal facility locations, energy sources, water consumption, and ownership chains. No public database exists. Planning disputes flare and pass with little systematic documentation. Ownership runs through REITs, sovereign wealth funds, and shell companies across multiple jurisdictions. The word "cloud" does ideological work: it naturalises infrastructure, makes it weightless, placeless.

Datacentering — the active, continuous socio-material process through which digital infrastructure is assembled, contested, and reproduced — names what "cloud" conceals. This project maps it across four dimensions: conceptual, visual, structural, temporal.

// 03   WP1 · Conceptual

Environmental Claim Index divergence

The ECI is a weighted keyword density score measuring how intensely an operator's corporate website deploys green language, relative to the Wikipedia article for the same city. High divergence = imaginary inflation: the operator narrates sustainability while the city article narrates planning disputes and energy demand.

Key finding · WP1 seed run, April 2026

AWS corporate ECI = 167.1 vs Wikipedia ECI = 1.4 — a gap of +165.7. Equinix's website returned zero content to the research scraper: WAF-blocked, itself recorded as a strategic opacity finding at the technical layer.

// 04   WP2 · Visual

Nighttime light growth & thermal signatures

VIIRS nighttime lights (2014–2024) reveal electricity-intensity growth around facility clusters. Ashburn shows the clearest signal: exurban context means less background noise from urban LED transitions. Amsterdam's apparent decrease reflects city-wide LED streetlight replacement masking data center growth — itself a finding about measurement confounds in urban vs exurban siting strategies.

Key finding · WP2 seed run, April 2026

Ashburn nighttime radiance: +8.7% (2014–2024) — only city with net increase. Amsterdam: −9.2% (LED transition masking effect). LST thermal maps show cooling-tower heat plumes visible from Landsat at 30m resolution.

// 05   WP3 · Structural

Ownership cartography & urban embeddedness

Who owns this infrastructure, in what legal form, with what consequences for governance? The REIT structure distributes beneficial ownership across retail investors and pension funds, dissolving the concentrated accountability that would otherwise attach to a single identifiable owner. When communities or municipalities seek a counterparty for binding commitments, the REIT provides none.

Key finding · WP3 seed run, April 2026

Ashburn: 36 power nodes, 1 residential zone, 0 adjacency edges — deliberate land-use segregation at scale. Amsterdam: 61 residential zones, 16 adjacency edges — embedded in the urban fabric. The structural difference, not signal strength, explains the governance divergence.

// 06   WP4 · Temporal

Contestation signals & regime response lag

58 documented contestation events (2007–2025) across four jurisdictions. Amsterdam declares a moratorium within months of signal onset in 2019. Northern Virginia produces no moratorium despite comparable cumulative signal. Dublin shows the highest sustained negativity — 81% negative-tone events — driven by EirGrid grid-capacity constraints and An Bord Pleanála refusals.

Key finding · WP4 seed corpus, April 2026

Signal onset: Amsterdam 2019, Dublin 2020, Ashburn 2021. Amsterdam signal-to-response lag: < 1 year. Ashburn lag: undefined — no regime response at end of observation window (2025). The lag variable operationalises WP3's governance counterparty finding.

// 07   Contribute

The cartography is a commons

Datacentering is too large, too distributed, too opaque for any single team. The infrastructure is deliberately fragmented across jurisdictions. The research commons is the methodological response.

[01]
Add a facility
Add a record to the facility index CSV. Coordinates, operator name, city, and any notes on planning history or contestation. No code required.
[02]
Local knowledge
Know about a planning dispute, a moratorium, a community campaign, or an unreported facility? Open a GitHub Discussion. That knowledge is valuable here.
[03]
Data access
Company registry access, REIT filings, planning portal scraping rights, or MediaCloud/GDELT API credentials for non-English jurisdictions.
[04]
Replicate in other languages
The WP1 ECI pipeline and WP4 contestation corpus are language-agnostic. Replication in French, German, Dutch, Japanese, or Chinese would be immediately valuable.
