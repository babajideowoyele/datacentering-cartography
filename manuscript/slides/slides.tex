\vspace{0.5em}

%% SLIDE 4 — Introducing Datacentering
\begin{frame}{Introducing ``Datacentering''}

\begin{block}{A conceptual reframing}
\textbf{Datacentering} = the active socio-material process through which digital infrastructure is continuously assembled, contested, and reproduced
\end{block}

\vspace{0.8em}
\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
\textbf{Why a gerund?}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Foregrounds \textit{process} over static object
  \item Makes actors visible: who is doing the datacentering?
  \item Makes contestation visible: what is being assembled or foreclosed?
\end{itemize}
\end{column}
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
\textbf{Theoretical grounding}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Orlikowski (2007): sociomaterial entanglement
  \item Jasanoff \& Kim (2015): imaginaries co-produce material facts
  \item The ``cloud'' is an imaginary that suppresses what datacentering names
\end{itemize}
\end{column}
\end{columns}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{A Multimodal Research Agenda}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
\textbf{WP} & \textbf{CAS Dimension} & \textbf{Data} & \textbf{Output} \\
\midrule
WP1 & Conceptual & Operator websites, Wikipedia & Imaginary divergence \\
WP2 & Conceptual & Satellite + corporate imagery & Abstraction score \\
WP3 & Structural & OpenCorporates, REIT filings & Ownership network \\
WP4 & Temporal & News, planning records, social media & Signal-to-response lag \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\vspace{0.5em}
\begin{block}{Why multimodal?}
Strategic opacity is a \textit{sociotechnical fact} — not a data gap. \\
Each dimension of the assemblage is unreachable by the methods suited to the others.\\
\small A firm that won't publish is still visible in a REIT filing, a Sentinel-2 image, and a planning record.
\end{block}

\end{frame}
