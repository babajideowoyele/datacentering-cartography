%% SLIDE 4 — Datacentering
\begin{frame}{\iconfull{dcblue}{server}\enspace Introducing ``Datacentering''}

\metroset{block=fill}
\begin{block}{}
  \centering
  \textbf{Datacentering} = the active, contested, socio-material process of
  assembling, operating, and reproducing digital infrastructure in place
\end{block}

\vspace{0.5em}
\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
  \icon{question-circle}\textbf{Why a gerund?}
  \begin{itemize}\small
    \item Process, not static object
    \item \textit{Who} is doing it? \textit{For whom}?
    \item What is assembled — and foreclosed?
  \end{itemize}
\end{column}
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
  \icon{layer-group}\textbf{Theoretical grounding}
  \begin{itemize}\small
    \item Orlikowski (2007): sociomaterial entanglement
    \item Jasanoff \& Kim (2015): imaginaries co-produce facts
    \item ``Cloud'' \textit{suppresses} what datacentering names
  \end{itemize}
\end{column}
\end{columns}
\note{Why a gerund? Because "data center" treats the thing as fixed and technical. "Datacentering" asks: who is doing this, for whom, under what conditions, and who gets to contest it? It's the same move STS makes with "technoscience" — the suffix matters because it names a process that can go otherwise.}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[noframenumbering]{\faIcon{question-circle}\enspace Q: Why CAS and not MLP / TIS / SNM?}
\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
  \small\textbf{The honest answer: they are complementary}\\[0.4em]
  \footnotesize
  MLP excels when a niche is visible and a regime is identifiable. Here:\\[0.3em]
  \begin{itemize}
    \item No stable niche — hyperscalers \textit{are} the incumbent
    \item No clear landscape pressure — it \textit{is} the landscape
    \item Regime boundaries dissolve across planning, energy, finance
  \end{itemize}
\end{column}
\begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
  \small\textbf{Why CAS as sensemaking lens}\\[0.4em]
  \footnotesize
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Emergent outcomes no single actor controls
    \item Non-linear feedback (moratorium → relocation → new contestation)
    \item Heterogeneous agents: grids, aquifers, pension funds, planning laws
    \item CAS doesn't replace MLP — it flags \textit{why} MLP categories keep slipping
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{0.3em}
  \textcolor{dcgray}{\textit{Future work: operationalise regime boundaries using the contestation corpus.}}
\end{column}
\end{columns}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[noframenumbering]{\faIcon{question-circle}\enspace Q: This is a research agenda — where's the actual finding?}
\small\textbf{Three findings, not zero}\\[0.4em]
\footnotesize
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Bibliometric finding}: 4,757 papers, zero MLP/TIS/SNM engagement. That is a finding — it characterises a field's blind spot with precision.
  \item \textbf{Empirical finding}: Amsterdam moratorium follows signal onset by $<$1 year; Virginia, with comparable signal intensity, produces no response. The difference is governance structure, not community mobilisation. That is a causal claim, not an agenda item.
  \item \textbf{Methodological finding}: ECI, ownership network, and contestation signal pipeline are demonstrated as tractable on real data — they \textit{can} be built, not merely proposed.
\end{enumerate}

\textcolor{dcgray}{\textit{The agenda is what comes next. The findings are the proof of concept.}}

  \small\textbf{The challenge is real}\\[0.4em]
  \footnotesize
  Crowd-sourced facility indices risk:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Operator-biased contributions (operators have incentive to shape their profile)
    \item Inconsistent verification standards across jurisdictions
    \item Coverage gaps in non-English-language markets
    \item Data decay (closures, ownership changes)
  \end{itemize}
\end{column}
\begin{column}{0.44\textwidth}
  \small\textbf{Governance mechanisms planned}\\[0.4em]
  \footnotesize
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Tiered sourcing: primary (planning docs, company registry) $>$ secondary (DCD, trade press) $>$ community
    \item Versioned, attributed edits (git-backed)
    \item Cross-validation against satellite imagery (WP2 pipeline)
    \item Editorial board: researchers, not operators
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{0.3em}
  \textcolor{dcgray}{\textit{Same QC challenge as Wikipedia — solved by protocol, not perfection.}}
\end{column}
