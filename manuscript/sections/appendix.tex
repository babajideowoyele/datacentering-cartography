% ============================================================

the three-operator facility index. The Environmental Claim Index (ECI) is a weighted keyword
density score; the divergence column (corp.\ minus wiki ECI) is the primary analytical signal.

--- the operator's website returned no content to the research scraper, consistent with a WAF

\section{WP3 Seed Results: Urban Embeddedness Graphs}
\label{app:wp3results}
% ============================================================

\Cref{tab:wp3results} reports the full output of the WP3 urban graph pipeline seed run
(April 2026). The pipeline downloads Overture Maps infrastructure and land-use layers
within a 2\,km bounding box of each facility and builds a heterogeneous NetworkX
\texttt{MultiDiGraph}. Edge types: \textit{near\_power} ($k$-nearest neighbours, $k=3$);
\textit{near\_water} ($k=3$); \textit{adjacent\_residential} (radius $\leq 1$\,km).

\Cref{tab:schema} describes each field. The index is maintained as a community-editable

\section{WP1 Keyword Vocabulary and ECI Weighting Scheme}
\label{app:vocabulary}
% ============================================================

The Environmental Claim Index (ECI) is computed as a weighted sum of keyword category
frequencies, normalised by token count. \Cref{tab:eci} documents the full keyword vocabulary
and category weights used in the seed run. Weights are provisional and will be calibrated
against a labelled validation sample in the full pipeline run. The vocabulary is
case-insensitive and applied after stopword removal; multi-word phrases are matched on
exact sequence before single-word fallback.
